Data shows that COVID-19 might be the cause for higher murder rates in NYC

By Aliyah Walker

November 13, 2020

Murders are on the rise in New York City. In October of 2020 murder rates have already surpassed the total amount recorded in 2019. There has been speculation around the city that these rates have risen due to the coronavirus pandemic.

Dr. Brian Lawton, a criminology professor who specializes in Public Health at CUNY’s John Jay College for Criminology, stated that COVID-19 might have something to do with the rise in homicides. “The victim/offender relationship here is key,” Dr. Lawton specified. Further stating that without having access to each “individual murders we need to be a bit cautious about making any sweeping statements.”

Dr. Lawton also stated that it is important to note that the city “has been at a historic low for homicide these past few years.” “We should always expect to see some fluctuation so a jump up may not be easily attributable to any one thing.”

This chart shows us the increase in murders over the past four years. Each number of monthly cases was added onto the last calculation to show us the change by month ending with the month of October.
